SIMULATION PROCEDURE
====================

The software runs a single static load case and evaluates three independent
failure modes per member, then linearly extrapolates to find the predicted
failure load.


1. LOAD CASE
------------
  Applied load        : 98.1 N total (= 10 kg x 9.81)
  Application points  : Nodes 5 and 21 (centre of bottom chord, both sides),
                        49 N each, vertical
  Direction           : -Y only (no live, dynamic, lateral, or eccentric
                        loads)
  Supports            : Nodes 1, 9, 17, 25 — fully pinned in X, Y, Z
                        (over-restrained, statically indeterminate)


2. ANALYSIS METHOD  (bridge/solver.py)
--------------------------------------
  - 3D direct stiffness method, pin-jointed bar elements (axial only,
    no moment transfer)
  - 3 DOF per node (Ux, Uy, Uz)
  - Linear elastic, small displacement — geometry does not update with
    deflection
  - Solve K_ff · U = F for free DOFs; back-substitute for member axial
    forces and reactions


3. FAILURE CHECKS  (per member, per analysis)
---------------------------------------------
  For each of the 88 members, the solver computes three safety factors:

    Check                  Formula                      Allowable
    ---------------------  ---------------------------  ------------------
    Tension                stress vs tensile strength   20 N/mm^2  (balsa)
    Compression (crush)    |stress| vs comp. strength   12 N/mm^2  (balsa)
    Euler buckling         P_cr = pi^2 * E * I_min / L^2   (compression
                                                            members only)

  I_min uses the weak axis of the rectangular section (no end-fixity
  factor, K = 1 — conservative pin-pin case).


4. PREDICTED FAILURE LOAD
-------------------------
  Forces scale linearly with applied load, so:

    failure_load_N  =  applied_load x min(SF over all members, all modes)
    failure_mode    =  whichever mode produced that minimum
    critical member =  the member at the minimum

  For the current design: 202 N predicted failure, governed by buckling
  of diagonal 3-12 (SF = 2.06x).


5. SCORE
--------
    score = failure_load_N / bridge_weight_grams

  Bridge weight = sum of per-member volume x balsa density (160 kg/m^3)
                  plus glue allowance (0.3 g per joint).


WHAT THE SIMULATION DOES NOT TEST
---------------------------------
  - Dynamic or impact loading
  - Lateral wind or eccentric loading
  - Joint failure (assumes joints are stronger than members)
  - Out-of-plane buckling beyond what weak-axis I captures
  - Non-linear (large-deflection / post-buckling) behaviour
  - Construction tolerances or material variability

The simulation is a single deterministic test case with three failure-mode
screens — failure is whichever member, in whichever mode, runs out of
capacity first as load is scaled up.
